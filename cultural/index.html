Highlighted specimens from other European Museums

Lagarta de Borboleta Monarca

Monarch butterflies have four or five generations and the last one is migratory. This generation is unable to survive the harsh winters of North America. Each autumn after stored the energy reserves required, begins a long journey of over 4000km, from Canada to the mountain ranges of Mexico
source: Natural History Museum of Lisbon

Crucian Carp

Crucian Carp is a fish of the family Cyprinidae, genus Carassius. Habitat: river, sea
source: Estonian Natural History Museum

Robberfly

Most of the fly specimens of the HNHM perished in a fire in 1956. Having been exhibited for the public, these large (5-6 cm) and impressive exotic robberflies luckily survived
source: Hungarian Natural History Museum

Featured Species

Giant orchid

Photo of the Giant orchid, Barlia robertiana, in bloom, in Crete, Greece. It is the biggest orchid of Greece. It can reach more than 500 cm in height. It grows in various habitats in Greece and the Mediterranean.
